Federal Reserve Economic Data


The F.R.E.D. provides quarterly data on interest rates and inflation rates. These metrics can be used to analyze the real estate market


A box plot is a great figure to show the numberical values of the prices of homes across America. According to our data collected, the box plot reveals housing price.

  • Median price of American homes lies around 1.2
  • The first and second quartile range 0.25 values from the median
  • Housing prices has several outliers reaching zero, revealing more inexpesive homes, and values reaching beyond 2.25, revealing more expensive homes.

FRED can also be manipulted into several scatter pots to show the relationship of regressors such as number of bedrooms, house age, average area income, population and more to provide outputs to those same variables. Most graph seem to show a lack of a linear relationship between their respective regressors and outputs.


This generate multi-colored scale plot is a heat map that uses a matrix of colors to represent the levels of correlation between dataset's variables.


Utilizing the same values, we can create several paired histograms to show if these values can be represented in other ways. Fortunately, we can visualize several normally distrubuted graphs for these relatationships such as average of rooms, or population area, price, and so on.


In conclusion, the FRED, sources several different data from federal housing datasets to provide to visuals for several qualitive relationships that are improtant to take into account when suggesting individuals to rent or buy based on real time housing data.
